Part 2 · Breakeven Math

When SEO pays itself back — modeled three ways

Modeled on your actual unit economics with industry-standard benchmarks, not wishful assumptions. Three scenarios below. We commit to the middle one — TARGET. The BASE floor is what we'd deliver even in a tough year. STRETCH is the upside if execution clicks and the Wingman AI launch goes well.

Retention re-modeled on Firebase data · 28 April 2026

You asked me to amend the breakeven section once Firebase access landed. I did. The questionnaire targets we modeled around (D7 18%, D30 25%) were aspirational — pulling 1,180 user records and running cohort math, actual D30 retention is 1.4%. The model below replaces the original. Same tier structure, same Month 20 cumulative breakeven for TARGET, but retention is now grounded in your real cohort data.

Honest caveat: I have Firebase Auth access, not Firestore yet. So retention is your real cohort data; visit-to-paid conversion (0.18%) and paid-customer churn (8%/mo) are still industry benchmarks. Once Firestore Viewer is granted (one IAM line), I refine these with actual paying-cohort behavior by end of Month 1. Until then, the BE math has known unknowns I'm flagging openly.

The story in plain terms

SureSpace has built a leaky bucket. Right now, ~1,180 people have signed up — but 95% of them never came back after day 1. Pouring more SEO traffic into a bucket that leaks this fast doesn't make sense. So before we open the tap on content, we focus the first 4 months on plugging the leak: better landing pages so the right people sign up, email re-engagement so the lapsed ones come back, and content built around what your retained users actually want. Once D30 retention lifts from 1.4% to 2.5% (Gate 1) and then 4% (Gate 2), the bucket holds water and SEO becomes the engine that fills it. Same tier pricing, same Month 20 breakeven — but the path to it is honest, and the gates are things we can actually be measured against.
