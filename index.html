Part 1 · Diagnostic

What we found on sure-space.com

A technically clean WordPress site that is content-poor and topically diluted. The fundamentals (speed, canonical, OG tags, Lighthouse 91-92 across all four pillars) are in place. What's killing organic performance is generic AI-written content, legacy pages from two prior site iterations, and missing app/FAQ schema for a business that is fundamentally an app.

1

Zombie content diluting topical authority

15-20 URLs from prior site iterations still indexed: a 5,500-word Bangkok nightlife article, a London wellbeing page, three abandoned Surespace-Jumeirah/Marina/Downtown location pages from 2025, and full WooCommerce shop/cart/checkout remnants from November 2024. Every one of these dilutes Google's understanding of what SureSpace is about.

2

AI-generated content with no E-E-A-T signals

Blog posts open with platitudes like "As the business hub of the world, the vibrant city of Dubai…" — generic, no named venues, no data, no author credentials. Bylines exist (Goldman Sarah, Jordan) but no author pages, LinkedIn, or credentials. Google's helpful-content updates target exactly this. It's why you rank below DR-37 competitors for KD-0 keywords.

3

Zero app/FAQ/Organization schema

Every landing page has a visible FAQ section. Every page talks about the app with App Store links. Yet there is no MobileApplication, FAQPage, Organization, or Article schema anywhere on the site. This is the single fastest AEO lever we could pull in Week 1.

Quoted from your own blog

We read your content before writing this. One example from /business-networking-events-dubai/:

Opening paragraph — unedited
"At Sure Space, we reinvent the experience of networking events in Dubai by professionals. We have constructed our social platform to suit the needs of people who prefer real friends and in-depth discussions…"

Grammar errors ("networking events in Dubai by professionals" is unidiomatic) and platitudes without specifics. This is textbook AI output. Rewriting three such pages with real Dubai venues, named event organizers, and actual dates is the single highest-leverage content intervention.

Part 3 · AI Search Visibility (AEO)

The window nobody is using

Generative search — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude — is reshaping how people discover services like SureSpace. Today, none of your Dubai competitors (including Meetup and Eventbrite) have optimized for this. That window won't stay open.

🎯 AI Overview spotted

The query "networking events dubai" (450 searches/month) now triggers Google's AI Overview above the organic results, with AI Overview Sitelinks pointing to cited sources. This is where the next year of clicks will be won and lost. None of the current top-10 competitors are structuring content to be cited. A schema + citation-capsule rewrite could win that AI Overview placement.

📊 SureSpace AI citation score

Based on structural analysis: 0 pages optimized for AI citation. No FAQ schema. No citation capsule blocks (2-3 sentence "quick answer" summaries that LLMs pull verbatim). No comparison tables. No fresh-date signals. No author entity schema. Every one of these is a known LLM citation trigger.

The asymmetric window

A DR-19 site that ships FAQ schema, citation capsules, and named-author article schema in Q2 2026 will earn LLM citations before a DR-94 competitor that hasn't bothered. LLMs have no memory of past authority — they cite whoever answers the question cleanest, right now. For SureSpace, this is a 6-12 month window where doing AEO correctly puts you on the same citation page as Meetup and Eventbrite in Perplexity / ChatGPT / Google AI Overview responses about Dubai networking.

1

Citation capsules on every page

Every landing page gets a 2-3 sentence "quick answer" block above the first H2. This is the single strongest LLM citation trigger we've measured. ChatGPT pulls these verbatim.

2

FAQ schema site-wide

Your pages already have FAQ sections. We mark them up with FAQPage JSON-LD. This alone wins you featured snippets and dramatically increases Perplexity-style citations.

3

Real author entities

Replace "Goldman Sarah" with named team members, LinkedIn profiles, bios, and Person schema. E-E-A-T is not a checkbox — it's a citation-decision signal.

A plausible path from 32 visits/month to 5,000 users

Phase 1 — Foundation

Clean up, schema, and three page rewrites

  • Redirect / remove 15 zombie URLs (Bangkok, London, shop/cart, old locations)
  • Fix the /networking-events-dubai 404
  • Ship MobileApplication, FAQPage, Organization, Article schema site-wide
  • Rewrite /meet-new-friends-dubai, /business-networking-events-dubai, /outdoor-activities-dubai with named Dubai venues + real data + named authors

Target: Move from 32 → 100-150 organic visits/mo. 2-3 rankings jump 30+ positions.

Month 3-6
Phase 2 — Velocity

Content cluster + authority building

  • Publish 2 genuine, named-venue articles per month (no AI sludge)

  • Earn 10 UAE-relevant backlinks from DR 40+ publishers
  • Launch proprietary data piece ("State of Networking in Dubai 2026") + pitch to UAE press
  • ASO alignment: match app store metadata with top-ranking keywords
  • Pre-launch SEO content for Wingman AI + Pro plan (timed to product launches)

Target: 300-500 organic visits/mo. First LLM citations in Perplexity + Google AI Overview.

Month 7-12
Phase 3 — Compounding

Category ownership + programmatic scale

  • Own "Dubai networking" + "Dubai community" category in search AND AI citations
  • Programmatic expansion: event-type × neighborhood pages (only where unique value exists)

  • Comparison content ("SureSpace vs Meetup Dubai", "Best friendship apps Dubai 2026")
  • Community-generated content: event recaps, user quotes, verified photography
  • Q4: Position for 2028 expansion (Abu Dhabi? Saudi? other GCC?)

Target: 1,500-3,000 organic visits/mo. Measurable contribution to the 5,000-user goal.
