Part 1 · The Semantic Ontology

Five layers, one idea

Most agencies start with a keyword list and reason upward. We start at L0 — the entity decision — and reason downward. This is why the pages we build compound in ways a keyword-first approach can't.

Why this architecture wins

A competitor with 2,000 flat blog posts loses to a site with 800 pages in hub-and-spoke architecture. Google rewards topical authority signal — demonstrated by depth within a cluster + clean linking between pillar/spoke/subtopic. Goldman's current approach: lots of pages, no hierarchy. Ours: fewer pages, better arranged, compounding faster.

Part 5 · Differentiation

How our content is different from Goldman's

The word "different" is doing a lot of work here. Pages per week isn't different — Goldman ships 5-10 pages per week. These are the specific *quality* differences that make our output compound and theirs stagnate.

TheProjectSEO Content
  • Named human authors with real bios, LinkedIn links, schema
  • Named Dubai venues, real events, dates, prices
  • Original data, quotes from real users or community managers
  • Citation capsules (LLM-ready 2-3 sentence answers)
  • FAQ schema on every page — engineered, not accidental
  • Internal linking to L2 subtopic + L1 pillar + 2-3 peer pages
  • UAE geo-tagged, hreflang en-AE
  • Content reviewed against anti-AI-detection checklist
  • Aligned to founder-stated positioning ("meaningful friendships")
  • Target: 1,200-2,500 words with depth, not fluff
Goldman's Current Content
  • AI-generated authors ("Goldman Sarah," "Jordan")
  • Generic references, no named venues or real events
  • No data, no quotes, no unique perspective
  • No citation capsules — LLMs can't pull clean answers
  • No FAQ schema (tracked 34-54 AIO keywords but unoptimized)
  • Random internal linking — no topical authority signal
  • 61% of impressions from US / UK / India (wrong audience)
  • Generic AI openers ("As the business hub of the world...")
  • Scattered across Dubai + London + Bangkok + designer apps
  • Target: 500-1,500 words, often template shell content
